Description:

This course introduces some of the basic microwave design principles and discusses their application in high-speed microwave and digital circuits. The course introduces transmission line theory and impedance matching techniques, microwave circuit network analysis and coupled line theory. It gives an overview of active and passive microwave components and discusses the design of practical microwave circuits such as low-noise, broadband and power amplifiers.

Te main focus of this course is on the on the application of microwave techniques in the design of real-world circuits and not on the sophisticated mathematics needed to solve Maxwell’s equations…

Overview of items covered in E6318:

  • Introduction

  • Transmission line theory, standing waves and transients on transmission lines

  • Smith Chart, impedance matching and transformation

  • Microwave transmission lines and discontinuities

  • S-parameter theory and applications

  • Passive lumped microwave components, microwave resonators

  • Coupled transmission line theory, microwave power combining and dividing

  • Microwave Power Gain, modern active microwave devices

  • Small-signal & low-noise amplifier design

  • Broadband amplifier design, balanced and distributed amplifiers

  • Nonlinear Microwave circuits, microwave power amplifiers

  • Transmission line effects in high-speed analog and digital systems

     

Catalog Description:  Introduction to microwave engineering and microwave circuit design. Transmission lines, Smith Chart, S-parameters, microwave impedance matching, transformation and power combining networks, active and passive microwave devices, S-parameter based design of RF and microwave amplifiers. Transmission line effects in high-speed digital systems. A microwave circuit design project (using microwave CAD) is an integral part of the course.

Modern computing and communications systems are operating at ever increasing clock or carrier frequencies. For engineers it has therefore become crucial to understand the basic principles and practical implications of designing “microwave-type“ circuits with dimensions a significant fraction of the signal wavelength. Additional microwave circuit design principles studied in this course and not found in typical analog or digital design courses include: single and coupled transmission line effects, the concept of lossless impedance transformation and power combining, designing circuits for maximum power gain or fixed input or output impedances, etc…

This course benefits not only the large community of commercial and government microwave system designers, but also high-speed analog, mixed-signal and digital designers.
