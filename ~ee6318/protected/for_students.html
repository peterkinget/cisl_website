Some useful microwave design links:

  •  Agilent Educator's Corner (http://www.educatorscorner.com)
  • After 1st class we'll take a look at the Transmission-Line Fundamentals Course from Agilent Educator's Corner (http://www.educatorscorner.com)
    When you have completed this course, you should have a basic knowledge of the technology, terminology, and measurement techniques of transmission lines. This course can be found in the RF Corner under RF Computer-Based Training Modules section. Please follow-up instructions to download and install this program.
  • If you browse around in the RF Corner  you'll also find some nice Jave animations featuring an animated Smith chart and the animation of a  Standing Wave along transmission line
  • Another great application note is the "classical" Hewlett Packard Application Note AN95-1. Can be downloaded as ZIP-file (one pdf-file and 2 QT-movies)
  • A very nice program to visualize transients in transmission lines, called Bounce can be found on the website of Professor C.W. Trueman at Concordia University, Montreal. Please download the program bounce.exe on your PC and take a look at the examples. We'll do small demo of this program in third lecture. 
  • To visualize steady-state propagation in transmission lines, another very nice program called TRline can be found on the website of Professor C.W. Trueman. Again, please download the program trline.exe on your PC and take a look at the examples as we'll also show it in third lecture. This program also allows you to see TL operation on Smith chart! (might be handy for checking the homework)

 

  • some 3-D movies of propagation in Waveguides can be found at the University of Ulm (explanation mixed German - mixed English). Open the zip-files, inside are animated GIF movies. In class we've seen wg.tlm, wgstep.tlm and wg90matched.tlm.
  • 2-D movies of different modes in waveguides can be found at the site of Hsiu C. Han at Iowa State University. Especially the parallel plate waveguide TE1 mode as function of frequency movie is interesting. Make sure you have Quicktime installed properly....

 

  • Microwaves101.com ("A practical Resource covering the fundamental principles of microwave design"...), pretty cool site, interesting "microwave encyclopedia" on most microwave components. Also the Microwave Calculator is worth taking a look!
  • Some very good tutorials on RF, RFIC, Microwave and Systems can be found on the RFIC.CO.UK website:
