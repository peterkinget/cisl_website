E6318 - Design Project - Spring Semester 2006 


Welcome to the design project webpage.!

What do I expect to find in the project report:

  • How you determined which transistor and if  applicable which bias to take for your application?
  • How you stabilized transistor (if necessary)
  • The design of matching networks (please include Smith Chart) matching networks are implemented using DUROID board
  • Which bias networks you took (explain maybe why)
  • Full implementation in ADS (schematic)
  • Final results in ADS:
  • for LNA: S-parameters, K-factor, noise figure,  power dissipation, P1dB and IIP3 of final design 

 

In the course of the design we will:

  • first, check the device performance of the available technologies: what is available gain, minimum achievable noise (if designing LNA), etc....

  • then implement a circuit topology, design appropriate matching networks (first using Smith Chart, please...!), design biasing networks to bias the device and guarantee out of band stability, finally trying to achieve circuit performance as close as possible to the ideal performance metrics

  • implement the layout of matching, biasing and if applicable combining networks on a high-frequency board (Duroid 5880) this involves calculating the dimensions of lines, couplers etc... and making a schematic drawing on how the layout will look like

Our design project will be performed in Agilent's ADS Advanced Design System.

Integration Technology

All designs will be using a common high-frequency board material to implement matching, biasing, combining networks etc...

As board material we will use: Rogers Duroid 5880, with a thickness of 20 mil (0.508mm) and an electroplated copper thickness of 1 oz. (37µm)

The dielectric constant of  Rogers Duroid 5880, is 2.20, relatively constant as a function of frequency, tan(d) varies slightly with frequency, take value at 10 GHz: 0.0009

As transmission lines you have the choice between either microstrip or stripline (using second substrate on top, which in practice might cause issue with active device, suppose you have cut-out there)

 

To calculate the characteristics of transmission lines on this substrate you can use ADS and optionally verify the obtained characteristics with  Rogers Corporation's Java-script program

 (http://www.rogerscorporation.com/mwu/mwi_java/Mwij_vp.html)
