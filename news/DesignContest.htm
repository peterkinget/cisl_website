CISL announces a

 

STUDENT DESIGN CONTEST

 

CISL is conducting a student design contest. The objective of this contest is to stimulate excitement in the field of circuits and their interaction with the physical world. The contest emphasizes the design of small, but elegant and ingenious, circuits. The design will require knowledge of electronics, power supplies, antennas, and radio reception. The circuit to be designed is a

 

SELF-POWERED RADIO

 

Basic idea: A radio is bombarded with signals from a multitude of stations, but it selects and plays only one of them. Can the energy provided by all the other stations be harvested and used to power the radio? One does not, of course, expect that it will be possible to power a loudspeaker with normal signal levels in this way, under normal circumstances. It is not unreasonable to expect, though, that it should be possible to power an earphone at levels significantly higher than those in plain, passive crystal receivers, and that other aspects which suffer in those receivers (such as selectivity) can be improved. To allow completion in a reasonable amount of time, the radio is to receive programs in the AM band, but the principles developed should be valid for higher frequency operation as well.

 

Prizes: Up to three prizes will be awarded, each of up to $1,000. The prizes are courtesy of the Department of Electrical Engineering. The designs will be judged relative to the contestants’ backgrounds.

 

Eligibility: The contest is open to Columbia Electrical Engineering or Computer Engineering students, both undergraduate and graduate. If several students collaborate on an award-winning design, the award will be shared among them.

 

Procedure and Deadlines: Those interested in participating should e-mail Prof. Y. Tsividis, tsividis@ee.columbia.edu, or Prof. P. Kinget, kinget@ee.columbia.edu for more details. Students are welcome to use the newly established Student Projects Lab to work on their design.  Simple parts will be provided by the Lab. Students should submit their design, including a working prototype and supporting written material, by February 11, 2005. The committee reserves the right to interview the designer(s). The winners will be announced by February 25, 2005, and a date will be set for presentations to the department community by the designers.

 

Evaluation: The submitted designs will be judged by a panel of experts from the Department of Electrical Engineering, consisting of Drs. Kinget, Melville, Shepard, and Tsividis, who combine expertise in academia, industry, and the radio amateur field. The designs will be evaluated for measured and subjective performance in a variety of situations, as well as for ingenuity and elegance.
