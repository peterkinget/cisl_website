Asynchronous digital signal processing for the software radio

Description:

In this project, we are examining an aggressive asynchronous micropipelining with adaptive on-chip voltage scaling for stream-based signal processing applications (specifically signal processing for high-bandwidth software radio applications).

Selected publications:

  • Y. Li, G. Pauounakis, and K. L. Shepard, "High-throughput asynchronous datapath with software-controlled voltage scaling," Symposium on VLSI Circuits, 2003 (to appear)

    It is widely recognized that adaptive control of the power supply is one of the most effective variables to achieve energy-efficient computiton.  In this work, we describe the development of a high-performance asynchronous micropipelined datapath that provides robust interfaces across voltage domains, performing appropriate voltage level conversions and operating between stages with fanout-of-four delays differing by almost two orders of magnitude.  With software-specified throughput requirements, the power supply of the datapath is scaled from 2.5 V to 650 mV using an on-chip dc-dc conversion system that combines linear regulators and switched-capacitor power supplies.  Because of the asynchronous design style, the processor operates continuously during the voltage scaling transitions.
